Campus life grades: Party Scene B+, Safety B+, Campus Food B, Dorms B, overall Campus C+, Student Life B. NJIT is located in Newark, NJ, a large city.

Housing: on-campus housing is available and freshmen are not required to live on campus. About 22% of undergrads live in college housing. The average housing cost is $9,950 per year. In student polls, 41% say overall dorm quality is great and 46% say the dorms' social atmosphere is great.

Campus food: a meal plan is available and the average meal plan cost is $5,906 per year. When asked the best food options on campus, students said food truck (25%), GDS / Taco Bell / Subway (22%), and Giovanni's (13%). 52% of students highly rate the dining facilities.

Overall opinion of the school and campus community: 12% love everything and have a lot of campus pride, 59% like mostly everything but wish some things were different, 24% feel the community is okay and are just here for an education, and 5% have no school spirit.

Safety: 70% of students say they feel extremely safe and secure on campus. On campus police visibility, 52% say police are extremely present and willing to assist and 44% say they are there if needed but not overbearing. On peer pressure around drugs and alcohol, 47% say you can have a great social life without them and 41% say most people partake but no one judges you if you don't.

Varsity sports: 61% of students say sporting events are attended but not a huge part of campus life, while 23% say varsity sports are a big part of campus life. Men's and women's varsity sports include Basketball, Soccer, Swimming, Tennis, Fencing, Lacrosse, Volleyball, Beach Volleyball, Diving, and Track and Field.

Clubs and activities offered include Campus Ministries, Dance, Drama/Theater, Film Society, International Student Organizations, Literary Magazine, Newspaper, Radio Station, Student Government, Television Station, and Yearbook, plus music groups like Jazz Band, Musical Theater, Music Ensembles, and Pep Band.

Favorite campus events and traditions: Highlander Day (24%), Renaissance Fair (20%), formals and events hosted by clubs (18%), movie night (17%), and Midnight Breakfast before finals (12%).

Party scene and Greek life: 32% of students say there are some decent frat/house parties on weekends only, 29% say "parties? what parties?", and 16% say there are lots of options Wednesday through Saturday. The biggest social event of the year is Theta Chi's Paint Party (23%). Only 6% of men are in fraternities and 6% of women are in sororities, and 57% say Greek life is average with no one treating you differently if you don't join.

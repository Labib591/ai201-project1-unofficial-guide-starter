Rating: 5.0/5 | Freshman. I like NJIT because it's a technology school, where almost every student is required to bring a computer for their intended degree. As a result, I feel very comfortable walking around campus and being in a classroom, since I know the college is centered around technology and I have an interest in technology too. What I would like to see changed is the campus to look more modern, such as giving old brick buildings a new colorful paint job, and to have technology stand out more, such as campus electric scooters making travel around campus more convenient.

Rating: 4.0/5 | Sophomore. NJIT is a small campus compared to Rutgers Newark but it's advantageous because it is easier to go from one building to another. I really like how most buildings have a seating area kind of like a lounge. Some classrooms and lecture halls are really old and need renovation. Overall, I like the campus. It's really nice in spring when the weather is warm, and I enjoy sitting outside on the benches and eating lunch.

Rating: 5.0/5 | Architecture / Digital Design, Junior. So far, my time at NJIT has been a good one. I majored in architecture my first year, then switched to digital design the second, which I plan to keep until completion in 2027. For both of my first two years I dormed on campus and I really liked doing this. The design fields are taken really seriously at NJIT, so being close to our labs and studios helped a lot. I do wish they were more transparent with the additional costs of these majors - the costs of supplies are not detailed anywhere when applying and get dropped on you a week before a project. The dorming facilities meet my standards, the food is good enough, and I feel pretty safe on campus despite the harsh name Newark has made for itself. I look forward to going back in the fall.

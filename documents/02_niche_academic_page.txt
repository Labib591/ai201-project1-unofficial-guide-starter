Rating: 5.0/5 | Engineering, Junior. My overall experience with NJIT has been challenging but very rewarding, especially as an engineering student. The university has a strong focus on technical education, and that shows in both the coursework and the expectations from professors. Academically, NJIT pushes you to really understand concepts rather than just memorize them. Courses in engineering like mechanical or electrical can be intense, with a lot of problem solving, lab work, and projects. Classes such as thermodynamics, dynamics, and machine design require consistent effort, but they also help build real skills that are useful in industry. The hands-on labs and projects are one of the best parts because they connect theory to real applications. It's not the easiest path, but it prepares you well for real engineering work.
